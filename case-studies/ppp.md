# PPP — $500M in 2 Weeks
**American Express · Spring 2020**

## The Context

In March 2020, the U.S. Small Business Administration launched the Paycheck Protection Program — emergency forgivable loans to help small businesses survive COVID-19. Congress allocated $349 billion in the first round alone.

American Express was approved as one of the participating lenders. The urgency was real: small businesses were running out of cash. Every day of delay had direct human impact.

---

## The Constraint

This wasn't a project with months of planning, a product requirements document, or a phased rollout. It was a two-week window to stand up a functioning lending line of business on an existing payment platform that had never done this before.

I was Director of Engineering at the time. I had a team. The question was whether to manage from above or build alongside them.

I chose to build.

---

## The Execution

With a small group — just myself and a couple of engineers — I rolled up my sleeves and worked as a hands-on engineer. We had to make rapid, deliberate choices about what was essential and what could wait:

- What data fields were legally required for SBA reporting
- What integrations could be minimized or simplified without creating compliance risk
- What the failure modes were and how to handle them without a full QA cycle

Speed mattered more than process. The team delivered.

---

## The Outcome

- **$500M+ in PPP loans disbursed** to small businesses
- **New line of business launched in 14 days**
- Enabled Amex to fulfill its commitment as an SBA-approved lender during the most significant economic disruption in decades

---

## The Leadership Lesson

There are times when a Director should manage and times when a Director should build. The ability to recognize which mode the situation demands — and to actually execute in either — is what separates leaders who can move at crisis speed from those who cannot.

I knew when to set aside the organizational chart and write code alongside the team. That instinct, and the credibility to act on it, is something I built over years of staying technically grounded even as my scope grew.
